Getting Started

How does Homenesty protect my child?

Homenesty uses two layers of protection working together:

  • DNS filtering — Routes DNS queries through Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 for Families, which blocks malware and adult content before a connection is even made.
  • On-device content filter — Intercepts every web request on your child's device and checks it against 174,000+ blocked domains across 8 categories. This powers the dashboard, block counts, and AI summaries.

Do I need to install anything on my child's device?

Yes. During setup, Homenesty walks you through installing the DNS profile and content filter on your child's iPhone. Both require your approval as the parent — your child cannot remove them without your Face ID or passcode.

Does my child need an Apple account or a login?

No. Children never log in to Homenesty. Only you (the parent) sign in with your Apple ID. Your child's name is entered manually during setup.

DNS & Filtering

How do I turn off the DNS filter?Settings

Go to Settings → DNS Protection and toggle off "Cloudflare Family DNS". This removes the Cloudflare profile from your child's device. The on-device content filter and your custom blocks remain active.

Useful if Cloudflare is blocking a site you need, or if you're troubleshooting a connection issue. Turn it back on when done.

A website my child needs is being blocked — how do I allow it?Settings

Go to Settings → Allowed Websites and tap "Allow a Website". Enter the domain (e.g. khanacademy.org). Allowed domains bypass both Cloudflare and the on-device blocklist. Subdomains are included automatically. Swipe left to remove an exception.

How do I block a specific website?Settings

Go to Settings → Blocked Websites and tap "Block a Website". Enter the domain (e.g. tiktok.com). This applies to all children and blocks the domain even if the DNS filter is off. Swipe left to remove.

What are Emergency Contacts?Settings

Domains listed under Emergency Contacts are always reachable — even when you tap "Pause Internet". Use these for sites your child must always be able to reach, like a school portal or doctor's website.

What categories does Homenesty filter?

Eight categories, each toggled independently from the Dashboard:

  • Adult content
  • Violence
  • Drugs & alcohol
  • Gambling
  • Social media
  • Gaming
  • Malware & phishing
  • Streaming

Pause & Schedules

What does "Pause Internet" do?

The red Pause button on the Dashboard stops all web traffic on your child's device instantly — except for Emergency Contact domains. Tap "Resume Internet" to restore access. It takes effect in under a second.

Can I set a bedtime or school schedule?

Yes. Open a child's profile from Settings and configure their school start/end hours and school days. During non-allowed hours the filter automatically blocks all web access. Enable "Exam period" mode for extra restrictions during test weeks.

Rewards SystemPremium

How does the reward system work?

Create tasks (chores, reading, exercise) worth a set number of minutes. When your child completes a task, mark it done — they earn those minutes. Minutes can be redeemed for rewards from a catalogue you define, like 30 minutes of gaming time or a movie night.

Do tasks reset automatically?

Yes. Daily tasks reset each morning at midnight. Weekly tasks reset every Monday. You can also mark tasks complete manually at any time.

AI Daily SummaryPremium

Where does the AI summary run?

Entirely on your device using Apple's on-device Foundation Models (requires iOS 26 with Apple Intelligence enabled). No data is sent to any server. If your device doesn't support Apple Intelligence, Homenesty generates a template-based summary instead — still private, still on-device.

The summary card shows "Generating…" and never loads.

This usually means no child profile has been added yet, or Apple Intelligence is not enabled. Check iPhone Settings → Apple Intelligence & Siri. The summary is cached once generated and won't regenerate until the next day.

Privacy & Data

Does Homenesty see which websites my child visits?

No. The on-device filter never records URLs or hostnames. It only increments an anonymous counter per category (e.g. "+1 adult content blocked today"). These counters are stored locally and never leave the device.

Does Cloudflare see my child's browsing?

Cloudflare processes DNS queries (domain names only — not full URLs or page content) to decide whether to block a domain. Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 for Families does not log individual queries. See Cloudflare's privacy policy.

Is Homenesty COPPA compliant?

Yes. Homenesty does not collect any personal information from children. No account is created for the child. No data about the child is transmitted to any server. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Account & Subscription

How do I manage or cancel my subscription?

Open the App Store → tap your profile photo → Subscriptions → Homenesty. You can cancel or change your plan here. Cancellations take effect at the end of the current billing period.

I switched phones — how do I restore my subscription?

Open Homenesty, go to the paywall, and tap "Restore Purchases". Sign in with the same Apple ID used to purchase and your subscription will be restored automatically.

How do I contact support?

Email us at homenestyapp@gmail.com. We typically respond within 24 hours on business days.
