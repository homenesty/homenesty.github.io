3. iCloud Sync

To allow the parent's filtering rules, child profiles, and reward balances to stay consistent across multiple devices (e.g. an iPhone and iPad belonging to the parent), Homenesty uses Apple's iCloud Key-Value Store (NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore). This data is stored in your personal iCloud account and is governed by Apple's privacy policy. We cannot access your iCloud data — only your Apple ID can.

Raw browsing data and block event URLs are never synced — only configuration (rules, profiles, balances).

4. How Filtering Works — Technical Detail

Homenesty uses Apple's NEURLFilterProvider API (iOS 26) to filter URLs directly on the child's device. The filtering decision (allow or block) happens entirely on-device. The URL being evaluated is never transmitted to our servers or any third party.

When a URL is blocked, we increment a local counter for that content category (e.g. "adult: +1"). Only these aggregate counts are stored locally and used to generate your daily summary. The actual URL is discarded immediately after the allow/block decision.

For DNS-level filtering, Homenesty optionally installs a DNS profile on the child's device that routes DNS queries through Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 for Families (family.cloudflare-dns.com). This is Cloudflare's privacy-first family resolver, which blocks malware and adult content at the DNS level. Homenesty does not operate a DNS server and never receives your DNS queries. Cloudflare's handling of these queries is governed by Cloudflare's privacy policy — they commit to never selling or sharing your DNS data and publish annual transparency reports.

5. Children's Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

Homenesty is a tool for parents and is not directed at children. We comply fully with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) as updated in 2025.

  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.
  • All data relating to a child (name, age group, usage counts) is stored on the parent's device and, where iCloud sync is enabled, in the parent's iCloud account. It is never transmitted to our servers.
  • Child data is never shared with third parties, used for advertising, or used for analytics.
  • We do not build behavioural profiles of children.
  • Parents may delete all child data at any time via Settings → Data & Privacy → Clear All Logs, or by deleting the app.
  • Persistent identifiers for children are opaque device-level identifiers generated locally and are never linked to a child's name or email on our servers.

If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately at nbalmu@gmail.com and we will delete it within 72 hours.

6. How We Use Data

The data we store is used exclusively to:

  • Enforce the content filtering rules you configure
  • Generate your daily summary ("3 adult sites blocked today")
  • Send local notifications about blocking activity
  • Manage your child's screen time reward balance
  • Sync your settings across your own devices via iCloud
  • Diagnose crashes and app errors (anonymised, Release builds only)

We do not use any data for advertising, marketing profiling, or sale to third parties — ever.

7. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with any third party, with the following narrow exceptions:

  • Apple — Authentication via Sign in with Apple (opaque user ID only) and iCloud Key-Value Store for settings sync. Apple's privacy policy applies.
  • Cloudflare — If the optional DNS profile is installed, DNS queries from the child's device are sent to Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 for Families. Cloudflare's privacy policy applies. They do not log or sell DNS query data.
  • Sentry — Anonymised crash reports in Release builds. No PII, no URLs, no browsing data is included. Sentry's privacy policy applies.
  • Legal requirements — We may disclose information if required by law. Given that we hold no browsing data on our servers, such a request would yield no meaningful information about your family's online activity.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

All data is stored on your device and in your iCloud account, under your control:

  • Daily aggregate logs are automatically cleared after 30 days by default.
  • You can manually clear all logs at any time: Settings → Data & Privacy → Clear All Logs.
  • Deleting the Homenesty app removes all locally stored data immediately.
  • iCloud KV Store data can be cleared by signing out of iCloud or deleting the app on all devices.
  • Signing out removes your account link. Child profiles remain on-device until you delete the app.

9. Security

On-device content filtering uses Apple's NEURLFilterProvider — all filtering decisions happen locally, never touching a server. If the optional DNS profile is installed, queries are encrypted using DNS-over-HTTPS (DoH) before being sent to Cloudflare's servers. Filtering rules and child profiles are stored in Apple's secure App Group container and iCloud, both protected by iOS device encryption and your Apple ID credentials. We do not operate a database containing your family's data.

10. Third-Party SDKs

Homenesty uses the following third-party or platform services:

  • Apple FamilyControls / NetworkExtension / UserNotifications / StoreKit — Apple first-party frameworks, governed by Apple's privacy policy.
  • Apple iCloud Key-Value Store (NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore) — For settings sync across the parent's devices.
  • Sentry — Crash reporting in Release builds. Configured with sendDefaultPii = false; no user identifiers, no URLs, no browsing data are ever sent.
  • Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 for Families — Optional DNS resolver. Only used if you install the DNS profile during setup.

We do not include any advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, or tracking libraries.

12. Texas Residents — TDPSA Rights

If you are a Texas resident, the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA, effective July 1, 2024) provides you with additional rights:

  • The right to know whether we process your personal data.
  • The right to access your personal data.
  • The right to correct inaccuracies in your personal data.
  • The right to delete your personal data.
  • The right to obtain a portable copy of your personal data.
  • The right to opt out of the sale of personal data, targeted advertising, and profiling. We do none of these.

To exercise any of these rights, email nbalmu@gmail.com. We will respond within 45 days. If we decline your request, you may appeal by emailing us with "TDPSA Appeal" in the subject line. If your appeal is denied, you may contact the Texas Attorney General at texasattorneygeneral.gov.
